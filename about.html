My Story

Wanderer by
choice

I grew up in Lisbon, Portugal — a city of hills, fado, and the kind of golden light that makes every cobblestone look like a painting. I'd always felt the pull of somewhere else, the specific restlessness that comes from watching planes streak overhead and wondering where they're going. It took me until twenty-six to finally stop wondering and start booking. That first solo flight to Tokyo — terrifying, exhilarating, slightly turbulent — changed the entire trajectory of my life.

"I didn't find myself travelling. I found out I didn't need to — I was already here, just waiting for a window seat."

Two years and eight countries later, Wander With Suzy is what happens when a former graphic designer falls head-first into slow travel, local food, and the art of getting comfortably lost. I write honest, unhurried stories about the places I've stayed long enough to love: the hidden tascas of Mouraria, a ryokan in the Japanese Alps where I was the only guest, the morning the Marrakech souks felt like mine. This blog is less itinerary and more invitation — to pack lighter, stay longer, and eat whatever the person at the next table is having.
